Wathai Heritage
Tea Bungalow

Tranquility at the edge of the wilderness

3 Rooms Birding Paradise Gibbons Dibru Saikhowa

Wathai is where the tea estate meets the wild. Nestled in Limbuguri Tea Estate on the periphery of Dibru Saikhowa National Park — one of India's biodiversity hotspots — the bungalow offers a stay that is equal parts plantation retreat and wildlife sanctuary.

Wake before dawn, and a short walk through Barekuri village will bring you to the territory of the Hollock gibbon, India's endangered ape. More than 400 species of birds have been recorded in the surrounding wetlands and forests. Evenings are for stargazing, fresh tea, and stories around the dinner table. Three rooms, each with distinct character, offer an intimate escape for nature lovers and those seeking genuine solitude.
