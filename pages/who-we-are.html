Our Story

The Purvi
Discovery Story

Pioneering heritage stays in Assam since 2000. We offer experiences beyond accommodation, championing responsible tourism.

Our Journey

25 years of
sharing Assam with the world

2000

The Beginning

Purvi Discovery opens its first heritage bungalow in Upper Assam, introducing travelers to the concept of tea estate stays in Assam. At a time when the world knew little of Northeast India, we began telling its story.

2005

Chowkidinghee Opens

The Chowkidinghee Chang Bungalow joins the Purvi family, adding a second property and cementing the brand's position as the definitive heritage hospitality experience in Upper Assam.

2010

Community Tourism Pioneer

We formalize our community tourism partnerships — Singpho village visits, weaving centre tours, and the gibbon sanctuary walks that have since become signature Purvi experiences.

2015

Wathai Joins the Family

The Wathai Heritage Bungalow opens near Dibru Saikhowa National Park, expanding our wildlife and birding offerings and creating the three-bungalow circuit that defines the Purvi Discovery experience today.

2020

Digital Transformation

Despite global challenges, we invest in digital booking capabilities, a new website, and online experiences — ensuring guests can plan their Assam journey from anywhere in the world.

2026

The Next Chapter

As heritage travel grows globally, Purvi Discovery begins a new chapter — bringing our colonial garden archive aesthetic to the digital world, reaching travelers who didn't know Assam was on their bucket list.

Our Commitment

Responsible Tourism

Every stay at a Purvi Discovery bungalow contributes to the local economy, supports conservation, and celebrates the communities who call this land home. We believe travel should leave both the traveler and the place better than they found each other.

🌱

Environmental Stewardship

Solar energy, rainwater harvesting, organic waste composting, and plastic-free operations across all three properties.

👥

Community Partnership

Local employment at every level. Weaving centre revenue sharing. Singpho and tribal guide partnerships. Tourism that builds, not extracts.

🐤

Wildlife Conservation

Revenue from wildlife experiences funds gibbon conservation efforts in Barekuri village and supports Dibru Saikhowa National Park patrol teams.
