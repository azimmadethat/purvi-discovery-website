Beyond the Bungalow

Curated
Experiences

Each day unfolds at your pace. Walk the tea rows at dawn. Listen for gibbons at first light. End with a cup of the world's finest tea, poured by the hands that grew it.

Tea Tours

The Living Tea Garden

Walk the rows. Pluck the leaf. Taste the difference between a first flush and a second flush. Tea isn't just a drink here — it's a way of life.

Half Day

Tea • Estate Tour

Tea Estate Walk & Tasting

Walk through the tea rows with an estate worker as your guide. Learn to pluck, learn to grade, and end with a curated tasting session of five teas — each from a different harvest season.

Difficulty Enquire
Full Day

Tea • Cultural

Singpho Tribe & Ingthong Village

Visit Ingthong village, home to the Singpho people, who have lived alongside the tea plant for centuries. Share their tea traditions, hear their stories, and understand a culture that the rest of the world rarely sees.

Difficulty Enquire
Full Day

Tea • Heritage

Majuli — World's Largest River Island

A ferry crossing to Majuli, the world's largest inhabited river island, sitting in the Brahmaputra. Visit Vaishnavite monasteries, meet the mask-makers and satras, and taste the island's unique fermented rice beer.

Difficulty Enquire
Nature & Birding

The Birdwatcher's Paradise

Over 400 species recorded in Upper Assam. Hollock gibbons swinging overhead at dawn. The call of the great hornbill echoing through the forest. This is Northeast India at its wildest.

Half Day

Birding • Wildlife

Maguri Beel Wetlands

A vast wetland teeming with migratory and resident birds. Spot the Baikal teal, the white-bellied heron, and dozens of species that make this one of Assam's most important birding sites.

Easy • All ages Enquire
Half Day

Wildlife • Gibbons

Barekuri Gibbon Sanctuary Walk

Walk through Barekuri village at dawn — the territory of the endangered Hollock gibbon. With an expert local guide, you'll track these primates through the canopy while learning about the villagers who share their forest with them.

Moderate Enquire
Full Day

Wildlife • Nature

Dihing Patkai National Park

One of the last remaining lowland forests of its kind, Dihing Patkai is home to the clouded leopard, the Asian elephant, and over 500 species of plants. A full day immersed in one of India's most biodiverse regions.

Moderate-Strenuous Enquire
Adventure

On Horseback & On Foot

Dust-free trails through endless tea gardens. Golf courses that would make Augusta jealous. The Brahmaputra at your side. For those who want more than a slow walk.

2-3 Hours

Adventure • Horseback

Tea Garden Horse Ride

Ride along the banks of the Brahmaputra and through dust-free trails laid in lush green tea gardens. Experienced riders only — these are retired racehorses from the Retraining of Racehorses programme, spirited but well-mannered.

Experienced Riders Enquire
Half Day

Adventure • Golf

Tea Estate Golf

Some of the finest golf courses in Northeast India are woven through Upper Assam's tea estates. Polished by golfers from across the world, these courses offer a game unlike anything you've played — set against an endless green horizon.

All levels Enquire
Culture & Community

People & Traditions

The culture of Upper Assam is inseparable from its tea. Visit Singpho villages, watch mekhla chadar weavers at work, and share tea with the people who have lived alongside this plant for centuries.

Half Day

Culture • Handicrafts

Mekhla Chadars & Weaving Centres

Visit the weaving centres of Upper Assam and watch the traditional mekhla chadar — Assam's most iconic textile — being woven from natural fibres. Each piece takes weeks to complete. You'll never look at fabric the same way again.

Easy • All ages Enquire
Seasonal Calendar

When to Visit

Every season in Assam has its own magic. Here's what each time of year offers.

Mar–May
Spring

First flush tea. Warm days. Best for tea tours and birding.

Jun–Sep
Monsoon

Lush green. Rivers full. Dibru Saikhowa at its wildest.

Oct–Nov
Autumn

Second flush — the finest tea of the year. Pleasant weather.

Dec–Feb
Winter

Migratory birds arrive. Cool mornings, misty evenings. Bihu festivals.
